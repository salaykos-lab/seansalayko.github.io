Professional Introduction

A focused overview of Sean's role, internship timeline, and the scope of work completed at Milwaukee Tool.

Introduction

My name is Sean Salayko, and I am a Packaging Engineering Intern at Milwaukee Tool, working on the Power Tools Accessories team. Throughout my internship, I supported a wide range of packaging projects with a focus on design execution, cross-functional collaboration, and data-driven analysis.

In this role, I contributed to packaging design efforts, assisted with testing and documentation, and supported various project-related data mining and analysis tasks used to inform packaging decisions. I regularly collaborated with other packaging engineers, helping support active projects while also taking opportunities to lead select tasks and projects when appropriate.

Internship Timeline

January: Onboarding, tool training, and introduction to team workflows
February–April: Packaging design support, testing involvement, documentation, and data analysis
May: Increased ownership of packaging tasks and cross-functional collaboration
June: Project wrap-up, reflections, and portfolio preparation

Project Highlights

These projects represent packaging concept development, prototyping, supplier collaboration, structural design, and time-sensitive execution completed during the internship.

Project 1

Interactive Bit Holder

Developed an interactive clamshell packaging concept with a hands-on feature to improve product engagement and presentation, taking the concept from CAD to a physical prototype.

  • Designed the 3D clamshell package and interactive insert using SolidWorks and ArtiosCAD
  • Created insert card geometry to fit seamlessly within the clamshell
  • Prepared CAD files for physical production and prototyping
  • Manufactured the prototype using the Zünd and thermoforming processes
  • Collaborated with the project team to review fit, functionality, and concept execution

Project 2

Trimmer Line

Identified a more cost-effective and sustainable packaging solution for Milwaukee Tool's trimmer line assortment while clearly differentiating new SKUs and maintaining strong shelf appeal.

  • Developed multiple packaging concepts focused on cost reduction and sustainability
  • Explored alternative materials, structures, and formats
  • Worked with Product Marketing on differentiation and visual appeal
  • Created physical prototypes to compare concepts

Project 3

15 PC Bit Set

Developed a new packaging solution for a 15-piece bit set using an existing footprint while improving product presentation and aligning with Brand Marketing expectations.

  • Evaluated trap blister, single-face blister, and clamshell formats
  • Created concepts for all three structures within the existing footprint
  • Led concept development that resulted in selecting the trap blister
  • Built a physical prototype to present to Brand Marketing
  • Finalized the dieline and sent it to PRC for sample development
Blister Format Evaluation Physical Prototyping Dieline Creation Brand Marketing Collaboration
Project 4

Foam Insert

Designed and produced a custom foam insert for a Packout case to securely hold tools for shipment, balancing speed, protection, and organization within a three-day timeline.

  • Worked with the Concepting team to align on tool layout and protection needs
  • Converted tool and case files into 2D CAD for foam layout
  • Designed custom foam cut geometry for accurate fit
  • Cut and fabricated all inserts within a three-day turnaround
  • Verified tool fit and shipment readiness in the final Packout configuration

Workplace Experience

My day-to-day work during my internship varied a lot, which made the experience really unique. Some days were meetings-heavy, where I was working with my team and cross-functional groups to align on projects. Other days I spent most of my time in the lab concepting, designing, and building prototypes. There were also days focused on project documentation, organizing information, and reviewing data to support ongoing packaging work.

I had a good balance between working independently and collaborating with my team. Having both allowed me to take ownership of tasks while still learning from others and getting feedback along the way. Because priorities could change day-to-day, the environment was fast-paced and required me to stay flexible and organized.

What made this experience stand out was that no two days were ever the same. The variety of work kept things interesting and gave me exposure to many parts of the packaging development process, helping me better understand how everything comes together in a real engineering environment.

Industry Insights & Career Reflection

This section captures how the internship changed Sean's view of packaging engineering and the skills he developed along the way.

Throughout my time at Milwaukee Tool, my view of the packaging industry changed significantly. I learned that packaging engineers take full ownership of their projects and responsibilities, from early concepts all the way through execution. The role requires balancing multiple priorities at once, while still being accountable for decisions and outcomes.

The internship also showed me how fast-paced the packaging environment can be. Each project moved quickly, often requiring flexibility and strong communication to keep things on track. Through this experience, I grew both technically and professionally.

On the technical side, I gained experience using software tools such as ArtiosCAD, NX, and SolidWorks, as well as hands-on exposure to lab equipment including the Zünd cutter and various testing machines. Professionally, I developed important skills such as organization, time management, prioritization, and clear communication, all of which played a big role in handling a changing workload.

Overall, this internship helped confirm my interest in packaging engineering and showed me what it looks like to work in a real, fast-moving product development environment.
